Question 1.1¶

Which statistical test did you use to analyze the NYC subway data? Did you use a one-tail or a two-tail P value? What is the null hypothesis? What is your p-critical value?

In [ ]:
Answer:
    I did mann whitney one tailed U Test to analyze NYC subway data.
       
    Samples: x_withrain = Sample of entries with rain , x_withoutrain = Sample of entries without rain

    Population: mean_entries(population_withrain) or µ_withrain , mean_entries(population_withoutrain) or µ_withoutrain

    Null Hypothesis Ho: µ_withrain = µ_withoutrain 
    Description: The µ_withrain and µ_withoutrain are from the same population - 
    therefore results we see between x_withrain and x_witouthrain are by chance only.

    Alternative Hypothesis Ha: µ_withrain != µ_withoutrain 
    Description: The µ_withrain and µ_withoutrain come from different populations.
        
    The p-value is the probability of the difference we are noticing between x_withrain and x_withoutrain is by chance.
    P value : 0.019309634413792565
    P critical value =0.05
    The results of mann whitney U test suggests that probability of getting the mean difference by chance is .019, which is very low.
    Since the probability is less than .05 , we can conclude that it is significant to reject our null hypothesis
    and we can reject our null hypothesis that both the samples comes from the same population.

Question 4.1¶

From your analysis and interpretation of the data, do more people ride the NYC subway when it is raining or when it is not raining?

In [ ]:
Answer :
    Given the results from the Mann-Whitney U test (p-value: 0.019309634413792565), we can say that more people ride when there is rain. 
    It is important to note that simply looking at the means of both data sets is insufficient, due to variance. 
    The Mann-Whitney U test is needed to quantitatively confirm that the two data sets are statistically different.
